Lesson 1: Phonetics

Important concepts

  • Italian's born from the Latin language
  • We use the Latin alphabet
  • There are in total 26 letters
  • It's divided in 5 vowels and 21 consonants

Vowels

In italian there are some differences between phonetics and writing

The vowels:

  • a = /a/
  • i = /i/
  • u = /u/
  • e = /e/ (Closed)
  • e = /ɛ/ (Open)
  • o = /o/ (Closed)
  • o = /ɔ/ (Open)

Special cases

C

The letter "c" can be pronounced both as a "hard c" (k) or as a "soft c" (t͡ʃ)

In English, the two sounds can be found in words such as "skip" and "such"

Gemination

When we encounter a double consonant in Italian, we momentarely stop on the sound of the first letter

Examples:

  • Pizza
  • Matto
  • Palla
  • Bettazzoni

R

In Italian the letter "r" is "rolled" just like in Spanish

Examples:

  • Mare
  • Amore
  • Ramarro
  • Marrone

H

The letter "h" is completely mute in Italian, and it's used to differentiate two homophones on a writing level

Examples:

  • Ho - O
  • Ha - A
  • Hai - Ai
  • Hanno - Anno

The letter "h" is also used in combination with the letters "c" and "g" when followed by either "e" or "i" to differentiate between the hard and the soft sound

And now

some basic terms

Greetings

  • Hello/Goodbye (informal) = Ciao
  • Hello = Salve
  • Nice to meet you = Piacere
  • Good morning = Buongiorno
  • Good afternoon = Buon pomeriggio
  • Good evening = Buonasera
  • Good night = Buonanotte
  • Goodbye = Arrivederci
  • How are you? (informal) = Come va? / Come stai?

Manners

  • Yes = Sì
  • No = No
  • Thank you = Grazie
  • Thank you very much = Grazie mille
  • You're welcome = Prego
  • Please = Per favore / Per piacere
  • I'm sorry = Scusa
  • Excuse me (informal) = Scusa
  • Excuse me (formal) = Mi scusi

Directions

  • Left = Sinistra
  • Right = Destra
  • Up = Su
  • Down = Giù
  • Over = Sopra
  • Under = Sotto
  • Ahead = Davanti
  • Behind = Dietro
